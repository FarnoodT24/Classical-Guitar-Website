The Classical Guitar

Guitar History

15th Century: The Lute

It has often been claimed that the guitar is a development of the lute, which originated with a pear shaped body.

16th Century: Vihuela

fretted plucked Spanish string instrument that is tuned like a lute. Essentially a flat-backed lute

17th Century: Renaissance Guitar

Evolution into a figure-eight-shaped body from the application of design features and construction techniques for Spanish lutes.

18th Century: Baroque Guitar

Five double courses of gut strings and moveable gut frets. Included a more subtle body waist.

19th Century: Romantic Guitar

Most notable design changes include this guitar's six or more single courses of strings.

20th Century: Modern Classical Guitar

The final design. A model with a longer and wider body for greater resonance, and the revolutionary use of nylon and metal-wrapped nylon strings.
